HISTORY OF LIFI

Professor Harald Haas coined the term "Li-Fi" at his 2011 TED Global Talk where he introduced the idea of "wireless data from every light".He is Professor of Mobile Communications at the University of Edinburgh, and the co-founder of pureLiFi along with Dr Mostafa Afgani. The general term "visible light communication" (VLC), whose history dates back to the 1880s, includes any use of the visible light portion of the electromagnetic spectrum to transmit information. The D-Light project at Edinburgh's Institute for Digital Communications was funded from January 2010 to January 2012. Haas promoted this technology in his 2011 TED Global talk and helped start a company to market it.PureLiFi, formerly pureVLC, is an original equipment manufacturer (OEM) firm set up to commercialize Li-Fi products for integration with existing LED-lighting systems.

LIFI V/S WIFI

LiFi is a new high-speed wireless communication technology that uses visible light (LED lights) to transmit information. With LiFi, every light source in every office and home has the potential to transmit data. LiFi can be be up to 100 times faster than WiFi. This high speed and extremely stable internet is necessary for our transforming world.LiFi has a lot of benefits, such as increased security and speed, however, there’s a downside as well.
